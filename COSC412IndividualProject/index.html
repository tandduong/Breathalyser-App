Stay Safe On The Road

What is Breathalyser App?

Breathalyzer App is the Web-based Project developed to provide the tool of measuring the BAC value for everyone. It is also collect users' data for the purpose of further researching.

BAC stands for the Blood Alcohol Concentration, refers to the percent of alcohol (ethyl alcohol or ethanol) in a person's blood stream.

Read More about BAC

Why use Breathlyser App?

Using this app to keep track on our BAC, to reduce rate of traffic accidents caused by drunk driving, and save your money.

Get your BAC Result

The higher the BAC value, the better for you to NOT drive. The app will used your data such as your age, weight, type and amount of drink that consumed to calculate the final result. The advice will let user know if they should join the road.

Reduce Drunk Driving

Follow the advice to keep yourself from unwanted consequences and decrease the rate of drunk driving. Stay up late at the bar or party, or risk your own life on the road, that's your choice!

Save Money!

No one wants to spend any money on silly things. Choose this app as your partner in any party because it's free, safe and helpful. You can avoid paying for your emergency service, if you make wise decision.

From Zebra, 2020

Over a quarter (26.1%) of respondents said it takes 3-4 alcoholic drinks for them to feel unfit to drive, while 6.1% said it takes more than five.

The majority (30.8%) of people believe that after a DUI, a driver should lose their license but only temporarily. Interestingly, 28.1% of the total group believed it should take two DUIs to lose your license permanently.

In data comparable to last year's survey, 40.3% of people call a friend or a significant other to pick them up. 25.7% use rideshare, and a full 10% actually just walk home. These three options were also the most popular in last year's survey.

3.5% of respondents ages 25-34 prefer to use a motorized scooter service (i.e. Bird, Lime) to drive home after a night of drinking over rideshare.

Most people are aware of their limits regarding alcohol: 30.5% don't believe they have a high tolerance and won't drive after drinking.

18.9% of respondents admit to driving while buzzed. 2.1% admitted to driving while high, and 5.6% admitted to driving while buzzed and high. Remember buzzed driving is drunk driving and you can still face legal repercussions if pulled over while buzzed!

The estimation of the BAC is not entirely accurate because there are other factors affecting the results such as genetic factors, health status, daily habits or urinating. Therefore, to ensure 100% safety when participating in traffic it is best to NOT to drink or drink too much.
